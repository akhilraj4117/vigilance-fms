About the POSH Act

Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013

The POSH Act mandates every employer to constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) at every office or branch with 10 or more employees for redressal of complaints of sexual harassment.

Committee Requirements:
  • Presiding Officer - A senior woman employee
  • At least 2 members from employees committed to women's cause or having legal knowledge/experience
  • One external member from NGO or association committed to women's issues
  • At least half of the total members shall be women
Key Provisions:
  • Every organization with 10+ employees must have an ICC
  • ICC must complete inquiry within 90 days
  • Report must be submitted to employer within 10 days
  • Employer must act on recommendations within 60 days
  • Annual report must be filed by employer
Tenure: 3 years from the date of constitution Non-compliance can result in penalty up to Rs. 50,000

About the Disciplinary Action Complaints Committee

This committee handles complaints related to disciplinary proceedings against employees. It ensures fair and transparent handling of complaints as per the applicable service rules.

Responsibilities:
  • Receive and register complaints related to disciplinary matters
  • Conduct preliminary inquiry
  • Submit findings and recommendations
  • Ensure confidentiality of proceedings
Committee Composition:
  • Chairperson - Senior Officer
  • Members - Officers of appropriate rank
  • Secretary - For documentation

About the Suspension Review Committee

The Suspension Review Committee is responsible for periodically reviewing cases of employees who are under suspension. It ensures that suspension cases are reviewed timely and appropriate actions are taken as per rules.

Key Functions:
  • Review all pending suspension cases
  • Recommend continuation or revocation of suspension
  • Monitor progress of disciplinary proceedings
  • Ensure timely disposal of cases
Review Timeline:
  • Cases should be reviewed at regular intervals
  • Suspension beyond 3 months requires special review
  • Report to be submitted to competent authority
